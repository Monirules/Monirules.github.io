

# Monirul Islam Mahmud

Monirul is a researcher and a graduate student in Computer and Information Science at Fordham University, where he focuses on blockchain, applied cryptography, and machine learning. He currently serves as a Research Assistant at Fordham University, on projects involving blockchain based secure medical data sharing in wireless body area networks and developing zero-knowledge proof protocols for financial applications. 

Monirul also served as the Data Science team lead at the Design Inclusion & Access Lab (DIAL), where he spearheaded research on deepfake recognition and trustworthy diabetic foot ulcer detection. He also developed alternative credit scoring models that successfully increased loan approval rates for South Asian banking institutions by 20%. 

His technical expertise includes consensus mechanisms, distributed ledgers, and zero-knowledge proofs, alongside hands-on use of Python, Solidity, Interplanetary File System (IPFS), Hyperledger Fabric and cloud-based platforms. Looking ahead, he is open to research collaborations and opportunities in blockchain security, federated learning, machine learning and applied cryptography.
